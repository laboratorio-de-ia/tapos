Slide 1 – AI Governance Overview


Hello everyone.

Today I would like to share how we are building AI Governance in South America.

Our region includes several countries and many different business areas.

Because of this diversity, we needed a simple governance model that allows innovation while maintaining security, compliance and business value.

Our vision is not only to develop AI solutions.

Our goal is to create an environment where AI can be used responsibly, efficiently and at scale.

Our governance is based on four main pillars.

The first pillar is Governance.
It defines how decisions are made.

The second pillar is AI Platforms and Tools.
It provides employees with approved technologies.

The third pillar is AI Culture and Knowledge.
It focuses on education and adoption.

Finally, the fourth pillar is AI Use Cases.
It helps transform business ideas into real solutions.

Together, these four pillars create one complete AI ecosystem.


Slide 2 – Organization Structure

This slide shows how our organization is structured.

AI Governance starts with executive sponsorship.

Our executive sponsors define priorities and support strategic decisions.

The AI Governance Manager coordinates all governance activities across South America.

We also have AI experts who provide technical guidance and support project implementation.

One important part of our model is the Business Ambassador network.

Business Ambassadors represent different business functions.

They understand local business challenges and identify opportunities where AI can create value.

Working together with them, we have our Functional ICT teams.

They evaluate technical feasibility and support solution development.

Another important connection is with the Global Center of Excellence for Generative AI.

This collaboration allows us to align regional initiatives with global standards, best practices and corporate strategy.

In summary, our model combines business, technology and governance into one collaborative structure.


Slide 3 – Governance Committees



Governance is not only about approving projects.

It is also about creating transparency, reducing risks and supporting innovation.

For this reason, we created three governance committees.

The first committee is the Executive Committee.

This committee meets every quarter.

Its main responsibility is strategic decision-making.

It reviews important AI initiatives, monitors business results and defines priorities for the region.

The second committee is the Operative Committee.

This committee meets every month.

It works much closer to day-to-day activities.

The team supports AI literacy, evaluates new opportunities, helps teams use the approved AI platforms and follows project execution.

It is also responsible for sharing successful initiatives across different business areas.

The third committee is the Regulatory Committee.

This committee focuses on responsible AI.

It evaluates privacy, security, legal requirements and ethical risks.

It also monitors changes in AI regulations in different countries.

The objective is to make sure AI solutions are compliant before they are implemented.

Together, these three committees provide strategic direction, operational support and regulatory oversight.


Slide 4 – Business Ambassadors Network


One of the biggest success factors of our governance model is our Business Ambassador network.

Instead of having AI managed only by the IT organization, we involve people from every business area.

These ambassadors help identify business problems that AI can solve.

They also encourage collaboration between departments.

Another important responsibility is promoting AI adoption inside their teams.

To support this community, we created a central AI portal.

This portal contains governance documentation, AI news, training materials, videos, available AI tools and useful references.

It has become the main communication channel for AI inside South America.

Our objective is to make AI knowledge available to everyone, regardless of their technical background.


Slide 5 – AI Community and Knowledge Sharing



Technology alone is not enough.

People are the key to successful AI adoption.

Because of this, we invest heavily in knowledge sharing.

During the year we organize AI events for all employees.

Internal AI Ambassadors present solutions already implemented inside Stellantis.

These presentations allow other teams to learn from successful projects.

We also invite external companies and technology partners.

They share market trends, new technologies and practical experiences.

These sessions are open to the entire organization.

They help create curiosity, inspire innovation and increase AI maturity across the region.

Every event also creates new networking opportunities between business and technology teams.


Slide 6 – AI Portfolio and Results



This final slide shows our AI portfolio.

Today, South America has more than 280 AI initiatives registered in our inventory.

These initiatives cover almost every business function, including Manufacturing, Supply Chain, Engineering, Finance, Human Resources and Sales.

Each initiative follows a governance process from idea generation to production.

Some projects are already delivering business value.

Others are currently under development, proof of concept or business evaluation.

We also classify projects into two categories.

The first category is Self-Service AI.

These are solutions that employees can safely create using approved AI platforms.

The second category is Non Self-Service AI.

These projects usually require software development, integration or more advanced governance.

Having one centralized inventory gives us complete visibility of our AI landscape.

It allows us to monitor progress, prioritize investments, measure business impact and identify opportunities for collaboration between different teams.

Most importantly, it helps us avoid duplicated efforts and accelerate innovation across the region.
